Plant Care Guides

Read our comprehensive soil, fertilizer, and diagnostic tips for 10 common houseplants.

Easy

Aloe Vera

Aloe barbadensis miller

SunlightBright indirect sunlight
WaterInfrequently, every 2-3 weeks
SoilWell-draining sandy succulent mix
FertilizerOnce in spring (diluted nitrogen)
⚠️ Trouble: Soft brown leaves Reduce watering immediately. Let sandy soil dry out completely.
Very Easy

Snake Plant

Dracaena trifasciata

SunlightAdaptable, low to bright indirect
WaterLet dry completely (3-4 weeks)
SoilHighly porous succulent mix
FertilizerOnce in summer (low strength)
⚠️ Trouble: Drooping, yellow leaves Repot to dry soil. Ensure the pot has healthy drainage ports.
Medium

Peace Lily

Spathiphyllum

SunlightLow to medium indirect light
WaterKeep moist (water once a week)
SoilPeat-rich organic loamy mix
FertilizerBalanced liquid food monthly
⚠️ Trouble: Brown leaf tips Avoid tap water high in chlorine. Mist leaves to increase humidity.
Easy

Money Plant

Epipremnum aureum

SunlightBright indirect sunlight
WaterOnce a week (let dry between)
SoilStandard well-aerated soil
FertilizerDiluted organic liquid monthly
⚠️ Trouble: Pale or bleached leaves Relocate away from hot direct sunlight. Filter the rays.
Easy

Spider Plant

Chlorophytum comosum

SunlightBright indirect light
WaterOnce a week (let soil dry out)
SoilWell-draining loamy mix
FertilizerTwice a month in spring/summer
⚠️ Trouble: Salt buildup / crispy ends Flush soil with distilled water occasionally; clip dead leaf tips.
Medium

Tulsi (Holy Basil)

Ocimum tenuiflorum

SunlightFull direct sun (4-6 hours)
WaterDaily (keep moist, not wet)
SoilFertile organic loamy potting soil
FertilizerSeaweed compost every 4 weeks
⚠️ Trouble: Black stems & spotty leaves Protect from extreme cold weather and frost; check drainage holes.
Hard

Rose

Rosa

SunlightDirect sun (6+ hours daily)
WaterWater deeply 1-2 times a week
SoilRich, moist clayey loam
FertilizerHigh nitrogen & phosphorus monthly
⚠️ Trouble: White powdery coating Treat with neem oil. Keep foliage dry when watering roots.
Medium

Lavender

Lavandula

SunlightFull direct sun
WaterVery sparingly (drought-tolerant)
SoilAlkaline gravelly soil
FertilizerDo not feed (prefers poor soil)
⚠️ Trouble: Yellow stems at base Provide strong ventilation. Add pebbles to potting mixture.
Easy

Desert Cactus

Cactaceae

SunlightFull direct intense sun
WaterOnce a month (allow absolute dry)
SoilGritty sand with minimal organics
FertilizerOnce in active summer months
⚠️ Trouble: Shriveling or wrinkles Slightly increase water volume, but ensure rapid soil drainage.
Easy

Echeveria Succulent

Echeveria elegans

SunlightBright filtered sunlight
WaterWater when soil is dry (2-3 weeks)
SoilSucculent mix with coarse perlite
FertilizerDiluted liquid food once in summer
⚠️ Trouble: Stretching outwards (leggy) Reposition to a brighter window. Cut and replant stretched heads.
